Kova Pay — Long-Term Build Plan

From telling you the card
to being the card

One Kova card in Apple Pay / Google Wallet. Every tap gets routed, in real time, to whichever of the user's cards earns the most at that merchant. This document maps how it gets built, what each phase must prove, and — because honesty is the house style — exactly which numbers we cannot know yet. The shape is known. Three numbers are not. Phase B exists to get them.

Why bother

Three things routing unlocks that advice never can

Zero-effort, literally

The core brand promise, completed. No glance at a widget, no memory required — the optimization happens inside the tap. This is the version of the product for the 95% who will never care enough to check an app.

Real transaction data — without bank login

Users pay through Kova, so the Recovered Rewards Ledger becomes automatic and exact, bonus tracking becomes real, and the privacy promise survives: Kova sees only its own transactions, because the user chose to make them through Kova.

A payments business, not an app

Interchange revenue on every swipe, a top-of-wallet position issuers can't ignore, and the dataset (real category-level spend of rewards optimizers) that the entire card industry wants. This is the "how it gets big" answer.

Architecture

Anatomy of one tap

The mechanics are proven — Curve has operated this model in the UK/EU for years. Kova's version, built on a US BaaS issuer-processor (Lithic, Marqeta, or Stripe Issuing class):

User taps the Kova card

Tokenized in Apple Pay / Google Wallet via push provisioning. One card, every merchant.

Auth request reaches Kova's processor

Merchant → acquirer → network → the issuer-processor holding Kova's card program.

JIT webhook → the routing engine

The processor asks Kova to approve or decline in real time (sub-3-second SLA). Merchant name + MCC + amount come in; the existing recommendation engine picks the winning card. This is the moment the whole company was built for.

Approve & settle

Kova approves; the merchant is paid through Kova's program; settlement T+1. Kova earns interchange on the front card.

Charge the underlying card

Kova charges the user's chosen card as a stored card-on-file transaction, passing merchant context in the descriptor (e.g. KOVA* TRADER JOE'S). This step carries the two hardest problems: category passthrough and funding cost.

Reconcile & ledger

Auto-entry in the Recovered Rewards Ledger. "Go back in time": re-route to a different underlying card within 30 days. Failed underlying charges = Kova's credit risk (dunning + retry + spending pause).

Longer term: Apple's NFC & SE platform (iOS 18.1+) allows authorized apps, via a licensed PSP partnership and commercial agreement with Apple, to run contactless payments natively — including becoming the user's default double-click payment app. Evaluated in Phase D, not before; Apple Pay tokenization covers the pilot.

The unit-economics problem, stated plainly

The number that decides whether Kova Pay is a business or a subsidized feature:

earn: front-card interchange ~1.0–1.8% (rails & mix dependent) pay: charging the underlying credit ~2.0–3.0% (card-not-present acceptance) ───────────────────────────────────────────── net: per routed dollar roughly −0.5% to −1.5% offset by: · Kova Pay subscription tier $10–15/mo · wholesale acquiring at volume negotiable, unknown today · rewards delta captured for the user avg ~2%+ per routed txn (why they'll pay)

Curve's answer to the same math was subscriptions plus FX revenue — and roughly £150M of accumulated losses getting there. Kova's discipline: the spread gets measured with real quotes in Phase B before a single line of Phase C is built. If subscription revenue per active router can't clear the net routing cost at modeled usage, the project parks. That's a kill criterion, not a footnote.

The Plan

Four phases, each with a gate and a kill switch

Phase A · Now → Gate 3 (runs inside the seed plan) — ~$0
  • Instrument routing demand: track the gap between recommendations shown and "used it" confirms — every unfollowed recommendation is a vote for automation
  • Add a "Kova Pay — join the waitlist" card in-app; waitlist size becomes Series A evidence
  • Write the regulatory scoping memo (2 weeks of specialist counsel, ~$10–15K, the only spend)
  • Keep the benefits DB schema routing-ready: MCC arrays are already first-class
Gate A
Proceed to Phase B only if the seed plan's Gate 3 passes (M3 retention ≥35%, revenue/engaged user ≥$10) AND the waitlist exceeds ~20% of MAU. Routing is an amplifier for a product people already use — never a rescue for one they don't.
Phase B · Series A months 0–4 — Diligence & the three numbers — ~$150–250K
  • Real quotes: 3+ BaaS issuer-processors and sponsor banks; acquiring costs for underlying charges; compute the actual net spread
  • MCC passthrough field test: route live transactions across the top 10 issuer cards via a prototype program or partner sandbox; measure which bonuses actually post
  • Counsel deliverables: program structure, staged-wallet network rules, state MT analysis, card agreement T&Cs
  • Hire the payments lead — someone who has shipped a card program before; this hire is half the phase
Kill criteria
Park the project if: net routing cost exceeds what a $15/mo tier covers at modeled usage; or passthrough fails on >40% of top-10 cards; or no credible sponsor bank will touch a rewards-routing program. Parking is cheap here; discovering these in Phase C costs $3M.
Phase C · Months 4–14 — Build & closed pilot — ~$2.5–4M
  • Virtual Kova card + Apple/Google push provisioning
  • JIT authorization service: routing engine answers the webhook inside the SLA, with a static-default fallback if the engine times out
  • Underlying-charge service: card-on-file vaulting, descriptors, retries, dunning, spend-pause on failed funding
  • Risk & compliance ops: KYC flow, fraud rules, dispute handling — headcount, not just code
  • Closed pilot: 1,000 users from the waitlist, Kova-Pay tier priced from day one (no free routing, ever — the economics forbid it)
  • "Go back in time" re-routing within 30 days
Gate C
Pilot proves: ≥60% of pilot users' card spend migrates through Kova Pay; funding-charge failure rate <2%; measured rewards delta per user ≥3× the subscription price; unit loss per routed dollar within the Phase B model. Miss badly on any → pause scale-up, fix or park.
Phase D · Months 14–24 — Scale decisions — sized by Gate C
  • Physical card (metal is the category's love language)
  • Anti-embarrassment backstop (Kova fronts a declined underlying charge) — only if credit risk modeling from pilot supports it
  • Evaluate Apple NFC & SE entitlement with the PSP partner: Kova as the user's default double-click payment app
  • Issuer relations program — convert hostility into partnership by proving Kova Pay grows category spend on well-matched cards
  • Wholesale acquiring renegotiation at volume — the lever that can flip the per-swipe spread
The end state
The advice product acquires and educates; Kova Pay converts the believers into a payments business with real transaction data, recurring revenue per swipe-month, and the top-of-wallet position. The app was the wedge. The card is the company.
